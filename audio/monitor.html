Monitor

Wired in-ear monitoring, belt pack style

  • $30 Berhinger Powerplay PM1 accepts 1 XLR in, has voluem knob, and accepts 3.5mm headphones. While there is no power source needed, this product is not designed for balanced line signals from a mixer. You need to connect an amplified source.
  • $50 Behringer Powerplay P1 accepts to 2 XLR ins, has knobs for volume and for balance, a switch for stereo/mono, accepts 3.5mm headphones. Powered by 9V or mains
  • $40 Behringer Powerplay P2 has a combo (XLR/TRS) input and volume knob. Runs on two AAA batteries

Wired in-ear monitoring, stand alone style

A guitarist/bassist (or even a keyboardist) might appreciate combining products below with this guitar and headphone cable.
  • $25 Behringer Micromon MA400
  • Rolls PM50s
  • Rolls PM351
The use of a headphone amplifier may be bypassed (by one performer) by using the headphone output. Note that output routing on the Behringer XR18 is a two-step process (headphone source is monitor, monitor source is, e.g., Bus 3) and that using Solo overrides the monitor source.
